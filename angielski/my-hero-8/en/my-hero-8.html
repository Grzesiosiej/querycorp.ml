Steve Jobs - one of the three founders of Apple Inc.



The beginning of the career:


In the fall of 1974, Jobs returned to California, where he attended Homebrew Computer Club meetings with Steve Wozniak. He started working at Atari, where he designed computer games together with Wozniak. At that time, it was discovered that the toy whistle
attached to the Cap'n Crunch breakfast cereal could make a sound at 2600Hz, a beep used by AT&T's long distance telephone system
. Jobs and Wozniak briefly in 1974 sold the so-called blue boxes based on this idea
to make calls for free. He planned to spend the earned money on a trip to India in order to achieve spiritual enlightenment



History of Apple

In 1976, together with Steve Wozniak, he founded Apple (about which an article will soon be written here).
Their first computer was the Apple I, sold for $666.66. In 1977 they built the Apple II, which was a successful
in the home computer market. Apple II made Apple one of the world's most important home computer companies
. In 1980, Apple entered the New York Stock Exchange, and Apple III also appeared on the market.


Come back

improved the financial health of the company. The iMac became the first computer to place a lot of emphasis on appearance (although otherwise it was also a powerful computer

Death of Steve


After working many times with Tim Cook due to health problems, he passed away at his home in California on October 5, 2011 around 3:00 p.m., due to the recurrence of previously treated pancreatic cancer and respiratory arrest. His death was announced on the Apple website.
